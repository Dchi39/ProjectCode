About the project

IOT based Bio-Inspired Capsicum Plant Analysis AI Model with Multi-Functional Autonomous Robot is designed to analyze and monitor the growth stages of capsicum plants while identifying potential diseases at each stage using computer vision techniques. The system employs machine learning algorithms to predict the next growth stage and forecast the types of diseases that could affect the plant, as well as estimate the time until the capsicum plant reaches the harvesting stage. The autonomous robot, equipped with a camera and sensors to measure temperature, humidity, and soil moisture levels around the plant, gathers essential data for real-time analysis and prediction. Additionally, the robot is equipped with a capsicum-picking mechanism, allowing it to assist with harvesting. This integrated approach ensures optimal plant care and enhances productivity by providing timely insights into plant health and growth progression.

Capsicum Plant

The capsicum pepper (Capsicum annuum) is the most popular vegetable of Sri Lanka having a wider coverage in all agro-climatic regions, specially dry and intermediate zones. Annually, it covers around 4000 hectares and produces nearly 36000 metric tones of the same. In case of local production which meets to the domestic demand at about 890 grams per capita, Capsicum is cultivated in all districts during both Yala and Maha seasons, while capsicum can be grown throughout the year in certain areas. NuwaraEliya, Badulla, Kurunegala, Puttalam and Anuradhapura are major cultivated districts.

Capsicum varieties around the world include several types, each with distinct characteristics. Bell peppers, also known as sweet peppers, are mild and come in colors like green, red, and yellow, commonly grown in the U.S., Mexico, and parts of Europe. Chili peppers, such as jalapeños and habaneros, are spicy and thrive in regions like Mexico, India, and Southeast Asia. Pimento peppers, which are sweet and aromatic, are widely grown in Spain and Hungary. Varieties like cayenne and paprika are used for spices, especially in South America and Eastern Europe. Capsicum types vary in heat levels due to capsaicin content, with hotter climates producing spicier varieties.

Capsicum plant growing factors

01 Climatic requirements/ Areas suitable for cultivation

Capsicum can be cultivated in nearly all agro-ecological zones of Sri Lanka throughout the year and can be successfully grown at elevations up to 1,500 meters above mean sea level (MSL). During dry periods, supplementary irrigation is required to ensure optimal growth.

02 Soil

Grows well in well drained, deep and fertile loam soils. Sandy soils also good when required amount of organic matter added. pH value between 5.5 -6.8 is preferred

03 Seed requirement

CA 8 – 1 kg/ha
HYW and LYW- 1.75 kg/ha

04 Nursery Management

3m x 90 cm and 15 cm height beds are prepared. Apply compost or cow dung 3-4 kg per square meter. Use burning, solar sterilization or agro chemicals to sterilize soils. Put seeds in rows 10-15 cm apart and less than to 1 cm depth. Apply a suitable mulch and provide irrigation. Seed germinate in 8-10 days. Seedlings are ready to plant in 21 days.

05 Land preparation

Plough the field up to 15-20 cm depth and two harrowings Raised beds have to be prepared for better drainage

06 Planting

Yala season – April and May
Maha season – November and December
It can be cultivated throughout the year under supplementary irrigation and also under rain shelters or protected houses.

07 Spacing

HYW and LYW – 15 x 30 cm between plants and rows
CA-8 and Gannoruwa Prarthana – 40 x 40 cm between plants and rows

08 Water supply

Irrigation to be practiced in 4-5 day intervals at early stages and 1 week interval at latter stages of the crop. This depends on the rainfall. Irrigation required at before and after fertilizer application, and during flowering and fruit development stages.

09 Weed Control

Weeding is required at 2,4 and 8 weeks after planting fertilizer can be applied after weeding. Soils can be added to the plants after weeding and fertilizer application

10 Harvesting

First harvesting can be done 75 days after nursery sowing and at 5-7 day intervals. Thereafter in 7-10 picks during the crop growth. Practice harvesting in dry weather conditions

11 Yield

10-15 t/ha – OP varities
20-25 t/ha – Hybrids
Higher yield can be obtained under better management practices

Main Diseases

Capsicum plants are susceptible to various diseases which can affect their growth, fruit production and quality. Here are some of the main diseases that affect capsicum plants.

Powdery mildew

This appears as white powdery patches on the upper surface of leaves. It can stunt plant growth and reduce fruit yield.

Verticillium wilt

This fungal disease causes wilting of leaves starting from the lower leaves and moving up the plant. The stems may also become discolored. Verticillium wilt can persist in the soil for many years.

Bacterial spot

This causes small, water-soaked spots on leaves, stems and fruits. The spots may become sunken and surrounded by a yellow halo.

Mosaic

This is the most characteristic symptom and it appears as a patchy pattern of light and dark green areas on the leaves.
